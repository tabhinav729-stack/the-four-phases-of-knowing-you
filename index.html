This space holds four poems.

Each one belongs to a phase — a beginning, a becoming, a bond, and something that still lingers.
Nothing to decode here.
Just moments that meant something, arranged gently in their own time.
Start wherever you wish.
You’ll find a bit of us in each.
for Payal.

The First Spark

In the hush before everything began, There was laughter too shy to stay, A glance that lingered longer than reason, And time learning to walk your way. Every word felt like discovery, Every silence—something said. You smiled, and the air remembered, That beginnings never really end. Maybe the universe sighed that night, Letting two stars bump into fate, And I—too lost to question why, Just learned what it means to wait.
“Moments have a way of finding what they were meant to become.”

The Keeper of Calm

There were days when silence spoke, Louder than our words could try, You steadied the storm I carried, With just a look, with just a sigh. You taught me that peace was real, That not all warmth must burn, Some love stays still and listens, Waiting for hearts to learn. And in your calm, I found my breath, A rhythm I’d almost lost — To care without demand or fear, Is love without the cost.
“Peace sometimes grows roots — and turns into something deeper.”

The Friend Who Stayed

You came quietly — no grand arrival, Just presence, gentle and near, And stayed long after reasons faded, Like a song that refuses to disappear. You knew the cracks and didn’t flinch, The storms and still held ground, You taught me that love’s truest form, Is sometimes friendship, profound.
“Sometimes, staying is louder than saying.”

The Quiet Kind of Love

It wasn’t fireworks — it was steady flame, Soft and patient, yet it grew, Not loud like stories people tell, But honest, constant, true. No promises carved in air, Just a glance that understood, That love, in its quietest form, Can still mean all that it should.
“And maybe that’s how forever begins — quietly.”

If you ever lose your calm,
remember — you are someone’s peace.

for Payal.
